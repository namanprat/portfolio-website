    Overview

    A platform for the financially illiterate

    The more financially independent I've become, the more difficult it has become for me to manage and track my expenses. It causes one to be irresponsible as it’s nobody really takes the initiative to teach an individual to be financially literate. To counteract this, I downloaded a slew of financing apps, but to my surprise, the majority of them left a bad taste in my mouth. No app improved my experience or had features that set it apart from the crowd. None of the apps had a easy to use budgeting system or had an efficient dashboard.
    As a college student, we are overwhelmed when you have a bank account but don’t know what exactly to do with it. money.me strives to solve financial illiteracy.

    Competitor analysis

    Finding Opportunity in the Market

    In order to get a solid understanding of how our competitors are doing in the market and to lay out a solid foundation, we did a competitor analysis which consisted of direct and indirect competitors.
    I found that there is no interest-picking option for the kids or parents, while onboarding in our competitors. And only one of our competitors had offline content. Two of our competitors had a progress tracker which also has limited capabilities.

    User research

    Creating a questionnaire

    These are some of the questions I asked:
    1. How do you keep track of expenses?
    2. How often do you impulse buy?
    3. Do you have more than 1 card?
    4.Suppose you are going out in a group. How do you split the bill?

    Finding opportunity in the market

    Key findings

    These are some of the key findings:
    1. The app has no option for users to find out how much they spend on a specific item, such as food, and there is no search option for users to find out how much they spend on food this month.
    2. The main function of the app is to enter income and expenses, but due to the poor user interface, it is unclear where to enter new income and expenses.
    3. Not able to alert user properly regarding overspending.
    4. People do not want to add another app in their repertoire to check all the time (create widget)
    5. Inefficient categorizing
